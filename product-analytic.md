# Product Analytics

Product Analytics is the practice of analyzing how users interact with your application to make data-driven decisions for product improvement.

Unlike Continuous Monitoring (which focuses on system health), Product Analytics focuses on user behavior and business success.

### 1. Basic Event Analysis
*   **What**: Analyze key user actions like "Signup", "Login", "Create Article", and "Favorite Article".
*   **Why**: Understand which features are being used most often and where users find the most value.

### 2. Funnel Analysis
*   **What**: Measure the conversion rate through critical paths.
    *   Example: Landing Page -> Register Page -> Successful Signup -> First Article Posted.
*   **Why**: Identify "leaky" parts of your app where users drop off so you can fix the UX.

### 3. User Journey Mapping
*   **What**: Analyze the sequence of pages a user visits during a single session.
*   **Why**: Discover common patterns and identify if users are getting "lost" in the navigation.

### 4. Retention Analysis
*   **What**: Measure how often users return to the app after their first visit (e.g., Day 7 and Day 30 retention).
*   **Why**: This is the "North Star" metric for product-market fit. If users don't come back, the product needs fundamental changes.

### 5. A/B Testing
*   **What**: Show two different versions of a feature to different groups of users.
    *   Example: Test a "Green" vs. "Blue" Follow button to see which one gets more clicks.
*   **Why**: Make scientific decisions instead of guessing what users like.

### 6. Session Recording & Heatmaps
*   **What**: Use tools like **Hotjar** or **Microsoft Clarity** to see where users scroll and click.
*   **Why**: Find UX friction points (e.g., a button that looks clickable but isn't).

### 7. Customer Segmentation
*   **What**: Group users by behavior or attributes (e.g., "Power Users" who post daily vs. "Lurkers" who only read).
*   **Why**: Tailor the product experience or marketing to specific types of users.
